// 01 — about

Designing in the browser, obsessing over the details.

about.me

I'm a frontend developer who believes the best interfaces feel inevitable — like they couldn't have been built any other way. My craft sits at the intersection of clean semantic markup, considered typography, and motion that earns its place rather than decorating for its own sake.

I specialise in translating ideas into responsive, accessible experiences built on durable fundamentals: HTML, CSS, and a deep respect for performance. Whether it's a landing page that needs to convert or a portfolio that needs to impress, I sweat the spacing, the easing curves, and the load time.

// 02 — skills

The toolkit behind every build.

A focused stack I know deeply, rather than a long list I barely touch.

HTML5

Semantic, accessible markup that's structured to mean something — not just to render something.

CSS3

Custom properties, layered cascades, and animation curves tuned by hand for natural motion.

Grid & Flexbox

Layout systems chosen deliberately — grid for structure, flex for flow — never one for everything.

Responsive Design

Fluid type with clamp(), container-aware breakpoints, and layouts that hold up at any width.

Animations

Keyframes, transforms and transitions composed with restraint — motion that explains, not distracts.

Accessibility

Keyboard focus, contrast ratios and ARIA used correctly — not bolted on after the fact.

Git & GitHub

Clean commit history, branch discipline, and version control as part of the design process.

Figma & UI Design

Design files I can read fluently — and translate into markup without losing intent.
